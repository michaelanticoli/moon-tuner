How the
Natal Phase Works
What is a natal lunar phase?
At the moment of your birth, the Moon occupied a specific position in its orbit around the Earth — a precise angle relative to the Sun. That angle determines your natal lunar phase. It is not your moon sign (which changes every 2–3 days), but the shape of the light at the moment you arrived.
What is a lunar return?
Once per month — approximately every 29.5 days — the Moon returns to the exact phase angle it held at your birth. This is your lunar return, also called your power day. For a Waxing Crescent native, this means the Moon reaches that same 45–90° separation from the Sun each month. This is when your natal energy is most accessible.

Key terms
Arc Year
The 12-month cycle from one birthday to the next, structured by your monthly lunar returns.
Power Day
Your monthly lunar return — the 24-hour window when natal phase energy is most accessible.
Eclipse Window
Months when an eclipse falls near your return — the energy is amplified and accelerated.
Amplitude
The geometric precision of your return — 100% means exact phase match, lower means slight variance.
